The fungus Ophiocordyceps unilateralis, more commmonly known as "zombie-ant fungus" survives in tropical ecosystems by infecting ants.
The host exhibits behavioral changes, causing convulsions that drop them from their nest for the forest floor which is suited for fungal growth.
The lifecycle finishes by the ant climbing onto a leaf, clamping down (death clamp), and dying.
